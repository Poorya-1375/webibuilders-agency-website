Brand Positioning Statement
Use this everywhere -        headline, pitch, bio, social:
"We don't start with a template. We start with a conversation. Every website we build is designed around your business, your customers, and your goals - then built to look unlike anyone else in your industry."  
  



Design Direction for Your Own Website
Your site is your portfolio piece #1. It needs to signal:
* Craft - attention to spacing, typography, and motion
* Personality -        not corporate, not generic, warm and confident
* Uniqueness - the layout itself should feel custom


Recommendations:
* Use a bold, expressive hero with large custom typography -  not a stock hero image
* Subtle scroll animations (fade-ins, parallax on text layers) show technical skill passively
* Dark and light section alternation to create visual rhythm
* A custom color palette that isn't the usual blue-and-white or black-and-white
* Micro-interactions on buttons and hover states
* No stock photography of laptops and handshakes - use your actual work, abstract shapes, or illustrated elements

Conversion Strategy: Turning Visitors into Clients
The 3 Types of Visitors (and how to serve each)
1. The "just browsing" visitor They don't know if they need you yet.➔Hook them with the
problem statement in the hero and the "how we're different" section.➔ Get them to the Work page to see proof.
2. The "I need a website" visitor They know what they want, they're comparing options.➔ Get them to Pricing fast. Make the Growth plan the obvious choice.➔ Show the free setup offer prominently.
3. The "I need leads, not just a website" visitor More sophisticated. They care about results. ➔
Lead them to the Growth+ Automation plan.➔The "Our Process" page builds their trust.


CTAs - Keep It One
Every page should have one primary CTA: "Start my project" or "Book a free call". Don't split attention with multiple competing buttons. Secondary links are fine, but the main button is always the same.
Sticky Navigation
Keep a [Start my project] button in the top navigation at all times. Visitors should never have to scroll back to find the entry point.


Exit Intent/ Scroll Trigger
After a visitor scrolls 70% of any page, show a subtle banner or floating button: "Ready to get started? Book a free 20-minute call."
  



SEO: Your Own Site
Since you offer SEO, your own site should model best practices.
* Title tags with local intent: "Custom Website Design for Small Businesses I [City]"
* Hl on homepage includes your service + location
* Meta descriptions on every page
* Google Business Profile linked and optimized
* Schema markup for local business
* Page speed optimized (your site should score 90+ on PageSpeed)
* Blog (optional but powerful): "Why template websites hurt local businesses", "What is website automation and do you need it?", etc.
